The Elder Futhark

The Elder Futhark is the oldest form of the runic alphabets, used by Germanic peoples from roughly the 2nd to the 8th century. Its 24 symbols are divided into three groups of eight, known as aettir.

Each rune carries a name, a phonetic sound, and a rich symbolic meaning rooted in Norse mythology and daily life — from wealth and strength to journey, harvest, and inheritance.

Rune of the Day brings this tradition to your pocket, offering a moment of reflection grounded in centuries of lore.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about how the app works and your privacy.

Does Rune of the Day collect my data?

No. The app is completely offline and collects no personal information. Your theme, notification time, and daily rune are stored only on your device. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

How is the daily rune chosen?

Each day's rune is selected deterministically from the current date, so every device running the app sees the same rune on the same day. The selection resets at your chosen unlock time.

What does "reversed" mean?

Like tarot cards, runes can be drawn upright or reversed (upside down). A reversed rune often suggests a different shade of meaning — not necessarily negative, but a different angle on the rune's themes. The app shows reversed runes visually rotated.

Can I change the notification time?

Yes. Open Settings and choose any time from 5:00 AM to 9:45 AM in 15-minute increments. Your daily reminder will fire at that time going forward.

Which platforms are supported?

Rune of the Day is available now on Google Play in early access. An iOS App Store release is coming soon. The app requires no internet connection after installation.

Is the app open source?

Yes! The source code is available on GitHub. Contributions, issues, and suggestions are welcome.
